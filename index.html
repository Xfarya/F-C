  • about me

    Without a doubt, purpose-driven environments motivate me most, whether that be big ideas, or smaller technical tasks. I come from a background in international development, and have volunteered, worked and/or studied in several places, most recently Greece, Iraq and Egypt. Learning to code first caught my attention when I would try to think of ways in which we could find solutions to various problems in the non-profit sector (ranging from data-entry, to translations, to efficiency, to communications, to accountability and advocacy!). I felt that many of these could be solved if we had more targeted apps that were dedicated to suit our purposes. I started learning HTML and CSS using Codecademy, and tried to encourage the beneficiaries to do so also. On reflection, a combination of a lack of confidence/motivation on their part, and a lack of know-how on ours, the idea ultimately floundered.

    Since then, my progress has been relatively organic, and it is at this stage I feel ready to delve much deeper to develop a more holistic understanding of the broad sector.
